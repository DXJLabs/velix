# Use Cases

VEIL is designed for agreements where two parties need more than a simple token transfer.

In many real workflows, participants must discuss terms, revise proposals, attach payment context, coordinate escrow, and confirm completion before a deal can be considered finished.

These activities may contain commercially or personally sensitive information.

VEIL provides a private Deal Room that keeps communication, negotiation, payment context, and deal progress connected in one place.

> **Current status:** The use cases below describe the intended product experience.
>
> VEIL remains in pre-production. A use case must not be described as fully supported in production until its complete private execution flow has been verified end to end.

## 1. Freelance and Service Agreements

A freelancer and a client often need to coordinate much more than the final payment.

Before work begins, they may need to agree on:

- project scope;
- price;
- delivery schedule;
- number of revisions;
- milestone structure;
- payment conditions;
- ownership or handover requirements;
- cancellation conditions.

This information is normally spread across messaging applications, documents, invoices, and wallet transactions.

### Example Scenario

Alice is a freelance developer.

Bob wants Alice to build a website for his business.

They need to discuss:

- the features to be delivered;
- the total price;
- the deadline;
- which milestones will be paid separately;
- what counts as completed work;
- when escrow funds can be released.

Alice may not want her rates, client list, delivery schedule, or commercial conversations exposed publicly.

Bob may not want his upcoming product, budget, or vendor relationship visible to unrelated observers.

### How VEIL Helps

Alice and Bob open one private Deal Room.

Inside the Deal Room, they can:

1. discuss the project requirements;
2. create an initial offer;
3. revise the price or deadline through a counter-offer;
4. accept the final terms;
5. attach a private payment memo to each milestone;
6. coordinate escrow readiness;
7. attach delivery references;
8. confirm completion;
9. review the complete history of the agreement.

### Product Value

VEIL helps both parties maintain:

- one clear active agreement;
- a private record of revisions;
- payment context connected to each milestone;
- understandable escrow progress;
- one activity history for the complete project.

### Sensitive Information Protected

The private Deal Room may protect context such as:

- freelancer rates;
- client identity;
- project details;
- deadlines;
- revision requests;
- payment purpose;
- milestone progress;
- delivery references.

## 2. Private Buyer and Seller Negotiation

Buyers and sellers often negotiate before making a payment.

They may discuss:

- price;
- quantity;
- product condition;
- shipping method;
- delivery date;
- refund expectations;
- escrow requirements;
- additional conditions.

A public transaction does not explain these terms, while an ordinary private chat does not keep them connected to the settlement process.

### Example Scenario

Alice wants to purchase digital equipment from Bob.

Bob proposes a price, but Alice requests a lower amount and a different delivery deadline.

They also agree that payment should remain in escrow until delivery is confirmed.

### How VEIL Helps

The participants can use a Deal Room to:

1. discuss the product privately;
2. create a structured offer;
3. submit a counter-offer;
4. identify the currently active terms;
5. accept or reject the proposal;
6. prepare a private payment memo;
7. coordinate escrow deposits;
8. record delivery confirmation;
9. release or cancel the escrow according to the agreed conditions.

### Product Value

The buyer and seller do not need to search through a long chat history to determine:

- the final price;
- the accepted deadline;
- which proposal replaced an older offer;
- whether the escrow is active;
- what must happen before release.

### Sensitive Information Protected

VEIL may reduce exposure of:

- customer relationships;
- negotiated pricing;
- purchase purpose;
- order details;
- delivery timing;
- payment conditions;
- dispute or cancellation context.

## 3. Marketplace Escrow

Online marketplaces need to coordinate trust between buyers and sellers.

Traditional marketplace systems often control the complete account, communication, payment, and dispute process.

Crypto-native marketplaces may provide onchain settlement, but private negotiation and understandable escrow coordination can still be missing.

### Example Scenario

A buyer wants to purchase a service through a Starknet marketplace.

The seller requires an initial deposit.

The buyer wants the remaining amount released only after the service is delivered.

Both parties need to understand:

- what was purchased;
- which offer was accepted;
- what each deposit represents;
- what delivery condition must be satisfied;
- when release becomes available.

### How VEIL Helps

A marketplace can use the VEIL Deal Room model to provide:

- private buyer–seller communication;
- structured offer negotiation;
- private order or payment references;
- escrow readiness states;
- deposit progress;
- delivery references;
- release coordination;
- a shared activity history.

### Product Value

VEIL can act as the private coordination layer around the marketplace transaction.

The marketplace does not need to rebuild every communication and negotiation flow from the beginning.

### Integration Possibility

A marketplace may use VEIL to support:

- private offers;
- seller counter-offers;
- order-specific Deal Rooms;
- escrow status displays;
- private payment memos;
- delivery references;
- settlement activity.

This integration remains part of the intended product direction and must not be presented as production-ready until implemented and verified.

## 4. Milestone-Based Work

Many agreements are completed through multiple milestones rather than one final payment.

Examples include:

- software development;
- design projects;
- research work;
- consulting;
- content production;
- audits;
- community management;
- infrastructure work.

Each milestone may have its own:

- requirements;
- deadline;
- proof of completion;
- payment amount;
- revision process;
- release condition.

### Example Scenario

A development project contains three milestones:

1. design and architecture;
2. working prototype;
3. final deployment and documentation.

The client wants each payment released only after the related milestone is reviewed.

### How VEIL Helps

The participants can keep the full project inside one Deal Room while preserving separate context for each milestone.

They can:

- discuss milestone requirements;
- record revised expectations;
- attach a payment memo;
- submit delivery references;
- coordinate review;
- track escrow readiness;
- record release or cancellation;
- continue to the next milestone without losing the previous history.

### Product Value

VEIL provides continuity across the entire project.

Both parties can see:

- which milestone is active;
- what has already been completed;
- which payment belongs to which milestone;
- what still requires action;
- why a payment was released.

The contributor relationship, internal budget, assignment details, or review discussion may not need to be publicly exposed.

### Example Scenario

A project hires a contributor to create technical documentation.

- required documents;
- deadline;
- payment amount;
- revision expectations;
- proof of delivery;
